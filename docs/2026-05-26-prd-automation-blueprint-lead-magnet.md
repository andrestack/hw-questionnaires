# PRD — Automation Blueprint Lead Magnet

## Context

A public-facing interactive form that acts as Hinterland Web's primary lead magnet. A cold visitor fills out 4 cards of questions about their business, submits, and within minutes receives a **personalised "Your Business Automation Blueprint"** in their inbox — a short, specific report generated by Claude AI that names their top 3 automation opportunities, estimates time saved, and rates complexity.

At the bottom of every Blueprint: a single CTA — "Want to see how this actually gets built? Let's talk for 20 minutes." → Calendly link.

**This replaces a static PDF checklist.** The personalisation is the value. A generic "10 automations for small business" gets ignored. "Here are 3 automations specific to a 4-person painting business drowning in quote follow-ups" gets read, saved, and forwarded.

**Primary conversion goal:** Blueprint email → 20-min discovery call → AIOS audit ($1,500)

**Deployment:** `blueprint.hinterlandweb.com` — linked from LinkedIn posts, bio, and future site pages.

## Question Set — 4 Cards

Short form by design. This is cold traffic — every extra card loses completions. Each card has a clear purpose.

### Card 1 — Your Business

1. **What type of business do you run?** — select, required
   - Trades (builder, plumber, electrician, painter, landscaper, etc.)
   - Real estate
   - Retail or hospitality
   - Professional services (accountant, lawyer, consultant, etc.)
   - Health or wellness
   - Creative (photographer, designer, videographer, etc.)
   - Other

2. **How many people in your team, including you?** — radio, required
   - Just me
   - 2–5
   - 6–20
   - 20+

3. **What's the single biggest thing eating your time right now?** — textarea, required
   *(placeholder: "Don't overthink it — first thing that comes to mind")*

---

### Card 2 — What You're Repeating

1. **Which of these do you do repeatedly every week?** — multi-select chips, required (pick all that apply)
   - Replying to the same enquiries
   - Chasing quotes, invoices, or payments
   - Scheduling, rescheduling, follow-ups
   - Re-entering data across tools
   - Writing the same emails or messages
   - Preparing reports or summaries
   - Managing bookings manually
   - Something else

2. **How many hours a week goes to admin and repetitive tasks?** — radio, required
   - Under 2 hours
   - 2–5 hours
   - 5–10 hours
   - 10–15 hours
   - 15+ hours (I feel this)

3. **What would you do with 5 extra hours a week?** — textarea, required
   *(placeholder: "Growth, sleep, family, another client — be honest")*

---

### Card 3 — Your Tools

1. **What tools do you currently use?** — multi-select chips, optional
   - Gmail / Outlook
   - Xero / MYOB / QuickBooks
   - Tradify / ServiceM8 / simPRO
   - HubSpot / another CRM
   - Google Sheets / Excel
   - Airtable
   - Slack / Teams
   - WhatsApp for business
   - Nothing consistent — mostly manual

2. **Do your tools talk to each other?** — radio, required
   - Yes, mostly
   - Some do, some don't
   - No — I re-enter data constantly

3. **Have you tried to automate anything in your business?** — radio, required
   - Yes, and it works
   - Tried but gave up
   - Not yet

---

### Card 4 — Where to Send Your Blueprint

1. **First name** — text, required
2. **Email address** — email, required *(placeholder: "Your Blueprint will arrive here within a few minutes")*

## Data Flow

```
Visitor fills form
       ↓
Next.js Server Action
       ↓
POST → n8n webhook  (all form answers as JSON)
       ↓
n8n:
  1. Calls Claude API (claude-sonnet-4-6) with answers + system prompt
     → Returns structured Blueprint JSON (3 automations + quick win)
  2. Renders Blueprint into branded HTML email template
  3. Sends Blueprint email to visitor
  4. Sends lead notification email to André
  5. Appends row to Google Sheet (Leads tab)
       ↓
Success → confirmation screen
Error   → inline error, allow retry without losing form state
```

## Claude API — System Prompt (in n8n)

The prompt is the product. n8n passes all form answers and instructs Claude to return structured JSON.

**System prompt (stored in n8n Code node or set node):**

```
You are an automation strategist for small businesses in Australia.

A business owner has just answered a short survey about their operations.
Your job is to analyse their answers and produce a personalised "Automation Blueprint" — 
3 specific, realistic automation opportunities ranked by impact.

Rules:
- Be specific. Name the actual task, not a vague category.
- Prioritise quick wins. The #1 automation should be achievable in under a day.
- Match complexity honestly. Don't recommend complex solutions to simple problems.
- Speak plainly. No jargon. Write like you're talking to a busy tradie or small business owner.
- Each automation must include: a name, a one-line description, estimated hours saved per week, 
  and a complexity rating (Simple / Intermediate / Advanced).
- Also include one "quick win" — something they could do this week manually to test the concept before automating.

Return valid JSON only. No explanation outside the JSON block.

Schema:
{
  "businessType": "string",
  "headline": "string (e.g. 'Here's where your time is going — and how to get it back')",
  "intro": "string (2-3 sentences, personal, based on their answers)",
  "automations": [
    {
      "rank": 1,
      "name": "string",
      "description": "string (1-2 sentences, specific to their situation)",
      "hoursSavedPerWeek": "string (e.g. '3–4 hours')",
      "complexity": "Simple | Intermediate | Advanced",
      "howItWorks": "string (1 sentence — the mechanic, plainly explained)"
    },
    { "rank": 2, ... },
    { "rank": 3, ... }
  ],
  "quickWin": "string (2-3 sentences — something they can try manually this week)",
  "closingLine": "string (1 sentence — warm, direct, not salesy)"
}
```

**User message (built dynamically in n8n):**

```
Business type: {businessType}
Team size: {teamSize}
Biggest time drain: {biggestTimeDrain}
Repeated tasks: {repeatedTasks}
Weekly admin hours: {weeklyAdminHours}
5 hours back would mean: {fiveHoursMeaning}
Current tools: {tools}
Tools integrated: {toolsIntegrated}
Previous automation attempts: {automationHistory}
Business name: {businessName} (if provided)
```

## UX Behaviour

- **Progress**: 4 dots at top, fills as steps complete
- **Transitions**: `document.startViewTransition()` — slide left on Next, slide right on Back
- **Validation**: Zod schema per card, validated on Next/Submit press
- **Back button**: Available from Card 2 onwards, preserves all state
- **Required vs optional**: Optionals labeled "(optional)" — no asterisks
- **Submit button label**: "Get My Blueprint" (not "Submit")
- **Mobile-first**: Form must feel native on phone — this is social traffic
- **No login, no account**: Zero friction. Name + email only at the end.

---

## Qualification Logic

None in the form. n8n handles it passively:

| Signal | n8n action |
|--------|-----------|
| Team size 6+ AND 10+ admin hours/week | Tag lead as `high-intent` in Sheet, add `[HIGH INTENT]` prefix to André's notification |
| Real estate or trades | Tag as `ICP match` |
| All others | Standard Blueprint + standard notification |

André sees the tag in his email and decides whether to follow up proactively (vs. waiting for them to book via Calendly).

## Verification

1. Fill form end-to-end — all 4 cards transition, Back preserves state
2. Submit → row appears in Google Sheet Leads tab with all fields + timestamp
3. Submit → Blueprint email arrives at entered address within 3 minutes
4. Blueprint email renders correctly on mobile (Gmail app, Apple Mail)
5. Blueprint content is specific to the submitted answers — not generic
6. Submit → André receives lead notification with all answers formatted
7. High-intent signals → `[HIGH INTENT]` prefix appears correctly in André's email
8. Required fields missing → Zod blocks progression
9. Submit with n8n webhook down → inline error, form state preserved

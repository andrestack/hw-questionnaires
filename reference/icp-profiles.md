# ICP Profiles — Ideal Client Profiles

*Reference these when writing outreach, scoping projects, or pitching.*

---

## Profile 1: The Busy Tradie

**Who:** Sole trader or small team in a trade (painter, plumber, electrician, landscaper, builder)
**Location:** Sunshine Coast / SE Queensland (for in-person) or remote
**Revenue:** $150K–$800K/year
**Digital maturity:** Low — probably a Facebook page and a phone number
**Pain:** Too busy doing the work to think about the business. Website is old or non-existent. Admin eats evenings.
**What they want:** More leads, less admin, to look more professional online
**What they'll actually pay for:** Something that saves them time or wins them jobs — not "AI"
**How to pitch:** "Your competitors are getting found online. You're not. Here's what that costs you."
**Example:** Kevin (painter, Crafted Finishes) — perfect fit. Gave clear brief, trusted the process, values quality.
**Price sensitivity:** Medium — $3,000 is real money but they'll spend it if they see ROI
**AIOS opportunity:** Invoice follow-ups, quote generation, customer update messages — easily automatable

## Profile 2: The Creative Professional

**Who:** Photographer, designer, videographer, artist
**Location:** Any (online-first business)
**Revenue:** $60K–$200K/year
**Digital maturity:** Medium — has Instagram, maybe a Squarespace site
**Pain:** Portfolio doesn't convert. Website looks cheap compared to their work. Admin (emails, contracts, invoices) takes too long.
**What they want:** A website that looks as good as their work and converts enquiries
**What they'll actually pay for:** Portfolio that generates bookings + automated inquiry handling
**How to pitch:** "Your work is great. Your website isn't. Let's fix that."
**Example:** Stewart (photographer) — warm lead, needs website
**Price sensitivity:** Higher — often undercharge themselves, need to see the ROI clearly
**AIOS opportunity:** Client onboarding emails, contract sending, booking automation, gallery delivery

## Profile 3: The Small Real Estate Agency

**Who:** Independent agency (not a franchise) with 2–10 staff
**Revenue:** Property management + sales commissions
**Digital maturity:** Medium — has a website, uses a CRM (usually Rex or PropertyMe), lots of paper/PDF processes
**Pain:** Document processing (Form 6, lease agreements), data entry between systems, manual compliance tracking
**What they want:** Fewer data entry errors, faster turnaround on paperwork, CRM that actually reflects reality
**What they'll actually pay for:** Automation that directly removes staff time on manual tasks
**Example:** Greenhouse Real Estate — returning client, Form 6 PDF extraction + Airtable CRM sync
**Price sensitivity:** Low — if it saves 10 hours/week of admin, $500/month is a no-brainer
**AIOS opportunity:** Document extraction, CRM sync, automated landlord/tenant communications, compliance reminders

## Profile 4: The Service Business Owner (AIOS Sweet Spot)

**Who:** Business with 3–15 staff, clear operations, drowning in admin
**Industries:** Interior design, events, hospitality, marketing agency, accountancy, consulting
**Revenue:** $500K–$3M/year
**Digital maturity:** Has tools (Slack, email, maybe a project management tool) but they don't talk to each other
**Pain:** Information silos, repetitive emails, manual reporting, no single view of the business
**What they want:** Everything in one place, automated where possible, to stop answering the same questions
**What they'll actually pay for:** A system that saves them 2 hours a day — that's a month of saved labour cost annually
**Example:** Wife's interior design business (Berlin renovation projects) — first AIOS test case
**Price sensitivity:** Low if value is clearly demonstrated — will pay $5,000–$10,000 for a full setup
**AIOS opportunity:** Full AIOS — context OS + data connections + daily brief + supplier/client comms automation

## Not Our Client

- Tech founders who want to own the stack and argue about architecture
- Startups that want "affordable" and "just get it done quickly"
- Anyone who starts with "we have a very limited budget"
- Corporate teams with procurement processes (too slow, too political)
- Businesses that aren't growing — AIOS accelerates, it doesn't rescue
